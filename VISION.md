# HelixForge Vision

## North star

Build the **sovereign work and discovery platform**: one user-owned foundation
for software, knowledge, workflows, decisions, markets, learning, finance,
research, and physical systems.

HelixForge succeeds when every important act can be:

- understood before it begins;
- given exact human or workload authority;
- watched honestly while it runs;
- stopped, retried, or recovered when it fails;
- moved to another machine or provider;
- independently checked after it finishes.

**Helix** means systems that become more useful as they connect.  
**Forge** means deliberate creation with visible craft, pressure, and proof.

## Category-defining thesis

1. **One foundation, real domains.** HelixCore owns identity, policy,
   capabilities, jobs, audit, evidence, operations, and recovery. Every product
   earns its name through a real domain model and engine.
2. **Sovereign by default.** Local and self-hosted use are first-class. Data,
   relationships, policies, history, and proof can leave with the user.
3. **Human authority.** Agents receive narrow, temporary capability leases.
   They cannot retrieve raw secrets, widen their own power, or approve their own
   high-impact work.
4. **Truthful operation.** Slow work shows progress. Unknown stays unknown.
   Release gates run fresh checks. Completion never happens silently.
5. **Recoverable work.** Ordinary deletion enters a 30-day bin. Durable jobs
   pause, resume, cancel, retry, and recover from real checkpoints.
6. **Progressive power.** A new user sees a calm useful product. Experts can
   reveal deeper systems without changing tools.
7. **Cross-platform by contract.** Windows, macOS, and Linux are equal build,
   test, packaging, migration, and recovery targets.
8. **Frontier without fantasy.** Scientific and high-stakes products separate
   evidence, inference, recommendation, approval, action, and proof.

These documents describe the destination. They do not claim that the current
prototype already provides the capabilities.

## Success signals

- A fresh machine can build, test, package, run, back up, restore, export, and
  verify the supported local system on Windows, macOS, and Linux.
- A person can complete a useful journey without a cloud account.
- A project keeps its identity when moved between folders or machines.
- Every agent action is bounded, visible, stoppable, reviewable, and provable.
- Every product has real domain logic, not a renamed generic service.
- A user can remove a project and revoke its bindings without harming shared
  resources used elsewhere.
- The system states what its evidence does and does not prove.
